Laplace Transform – An Introduction

The Laplace Transform is a powerful integral transform used to analyze linear time-invariant systems. It converts functions from the time domain into the complex frequency domain (s-domain), making differential equations easier to solve.

Definition

The Laplace Transform of a function \( f(t) \), defined for \( t \geq 0 \), is given by:

\( \displaystyle \mathcal{L}\{f(t)\} = F(s) = \int_0^{\infty} f(t)e^{-st} dt \)

Applications

  • Solving linear differential equations
  • Modeling and analyzing electrical circuits
  • Control systems and signal processing

Common Laplace Pairs

  • \( \mathcal{L}\{1\} = \frac{1}{s} \)
  • \( \mathcal{L}\{t^n\} = \frac{n!}{s^{n+1}} \)
  • \( \mathcal{L}\{e^{at}\} = \frac{1}{s-a} \)
